Verifying Heap's Law

Heap's law is an empirical finding about relationship between vocabulary size and number of tokens. Given M = Size of vocabulary in a corpus and T = Number of tokens in the corpus, their relationship is formulated as: M = kTb, where k typically lies within the interval [30, 100] and b ≈ 0.5.

Using the analysis result above (M = 53037, T = 683994), k is calculated as follow:

k = M / T b

   = 53037 / 683994 0.5

   = 64.1287544

The computed k lies within the interval [30, 100], as predicted by Heap's law.
